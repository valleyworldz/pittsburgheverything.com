# Moving to Pittsburgh: The Ultimate Guide (2025)

Congratulations on your decision to move to Pittsburgh! This vibrant city offers an unbeatable combination of affordability, culture, and quality of life. Whether you're relocating for work, education, or a fresh start, this comprehensive guide will help you navigate your move smoothly.

## üè† Housing & Neighborhoods

### Finding the Right Neighborhood

Pittsburgh's diverse neighborhoods offer something for everyone. Here's where different lifestyles thrive:

**For Young Professionals & Downtown Living:**
- **Downtown/Liberty Ave**: Urban energy, walkable, close to business districts
- **Shadyside**: Upscale, trendy, excellent restaurants and shopping
- **Lawrenceville**: Artsy, hip, great nightlife and galleries

**For Families:**
- **Squirrel Hill**: Jewish community, excellent schools, family-friendly
- **Regent Square**: Suburban feel, top-rated schools, community-oriented
- **Mt. Lebanon**: Affluent, excellent schools, suburban amenities

**For Students & Budget-Conscious:**
- **Oakland**: University area, affordable, vibrant student life
- **South Side**: Historic charm, affordable housing, growing food scene
- **Bloomfield**: Italian heritage, affordable, authentic neighborhoods

### Housing Costs (2025 Averages)

| Neighborhood | 1BR Apartment | 2BR Apartment | 3BR House |
|--------------|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| Downtown | $1,200-1,800 | $1,800-2,500 | N/A |
| Shadyside | $1,100-1,600 | $1,600-2,200 | $250,000+ |
| Lawrenceville | $900-1,400 | $1,300-1,900 | $220,000+ |
| Squirrel Hill | $800-1,200 | $1,200-1,800 | $180,000+ |
| Oakland | $700-1,100 | $1,000-1,500 | $150,000+ |

### Moving Tips
- **Summer moves** (June-August) are busiest and most expensive
- **Winter moves** (December-February) are cheapest but weather-dependent
- Book movers 4-6 weeks in advance
- Consider PODS or U-Pack for long-distance moves
- Local movers: College Hunks Hauling, Two Men and a Truck

## üìã Moving Checklist

### 8 Weeks Before
- [ ] Research neighborhoods and visit potential areas
- [ ] Start apartment/house hunting
- [ ] Get estimates from movers
- [ ] Begin decluttering and selling unwanted items

### 6 Weeks Before
- [ ] Sign lease or purchase agreement
- [ ] Book movers or reserve moving container
- [ ] Notify current landlord and utilities
- [ ] Change address with post office, bank, etc.

### 4 Weeks Before
- [ ] Confirm moving date and schedule
- [ ] Arrange for time off work
- [ ] Start packing non-essential items
- [ ] Update insurance policies

### 2 Weeks Before
- [ ] Pack remaining items
- [ ] Arrange pet/house-sitting if needed
- [ ] Confirm utility connections
- [ ] Pick up moving supplies

### Moving Day
- [ ] Be present for movers
- [ ] Keep important documents with you
- [ ] Check inventory of items
- [ ] Pay movers upon completion

### First Week
- [ ] Register vehicle if needed
- [ ] Update driver's license
- [ ] Register to vote
- [ ] Get Pittsburgh library card
